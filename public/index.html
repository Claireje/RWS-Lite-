What is background radiation?

Background radiation is everywhere — it comes from the ground, the air, space, and even the materials buildings are made of. The numbers you see here are completely normal and not harmful.

Typical readings in Ann Arbor

Winter Dec – Feb
70 – 140 nSv/h

Windows shut, radon builds up inside

Spring Mar – May
60 – 120 nSv/h

Buildings start opening up again

Summer Jun – Aug
55 – 110 nSv/h

Usually the lowest readings of the year

Fall Sep – Nov
65 – 125 nSv/h

Starts climbing as buildings close back up

Why does it change?

☁ Cosmic rays hit harder in winter when the atmosphere above is thinner and less shielding.
⌂ Radon gas seeps up from soil and collects indoors when buildings are sealed in cold weather.
